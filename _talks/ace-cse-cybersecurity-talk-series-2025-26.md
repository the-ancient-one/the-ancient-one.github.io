I help organise the **ACE-CSE Cybersecurity Talk Series** at WMG, University of Warwick, where I lead **speaker engagement and relationship management** across the series. The programme brings together academic experts, industry practitioners, and senior security leaders to expose students to current ideas, emerging threats, professional practice, and career pathways across cyber security.

A key part of my contribution has been working closely with invited speakers, shaping engagement with external organisations, and helping ensure that each session offers students both technical depth and wider professional relevance. Through the series, students are able to hear directly from researchers, CISOs, technical leaders, strategists, and industry specialists on topics ranging from covert action and cyber diplomacy to business continuity, identity, post-quantum cryptography, SOC operations, and cyber risk.

### Technology and the Changing Nature of Covert Action
**Date:** 25 February 2026  
**Time:** 13:00–14:00  
**Location:** [IMC.004](https://link.mazemap.com/UTm7kRFN)

This session welcomed **Prof. Richard J. Aldrich, FRHistS**, Professor of International Security at the University of Warwick. The talk explored how modern science, technology, and cyber capabilities are reshaping covert action, examining performative assassinations, signature drone strikes, AI-driven disinformation, and online-recruited privateers. The session invited students to consider how advanced technologies may be accelerating new cycles of performative violence and destabilisation, while also blurring the boundaries between secrecy and spectacle, and between state and non-state actors.

Prof. Aldrich is a world-renowned expert in intelligence, security, and cyber communities. His work spans international security, cyber diplomacy, institutional memory, and intelligence studies, and he has advised governments internationally while contributing to major research initiatives in technology, security, and contemporary intelligence practice.

### Private Information Retrieval: post-quantum, but are we close to making it practical?
**Date:** 21 January 2026  
**Time:** 13:00–14:00  
**Location:** Online (MS Teams)

The ACE-CSE Cybersecurity Series opened Term 2 with a session led by **Sofía Celi** from Brave. The talk explored **Private Information Retrieval (PIR)** and the challenge of making privacy-preserving queries practical at scale, particularly in systems affected by computational overheads, dynamic data, and real-world deployment constraints. It also considered the relevance of PIR for recommendation systems and future directions towards practical, post-quantum-ready deployment.  

**Speaker Bio:**  
**Sofía Celi** is a Senior Cryptography and Security Researcher at Brave, specialising in privacy-enhancing technologies, post-quantum cryptography, and zero-knowledge proofs. They have contributed to the OTR messaging protocol, co-authored the MAYO post-quantum signature scheme, and hold leadership roles across the IETF, IRTF, and W3C. They also serve on the European Review Board at Black Hat, are an Honorary Industrial Fellow at the University of Bristol, and are a member of the Advisory Council at the Open Technology Fund. As co-founder of Criptolatinos and WinC, they are also a strong advocate for diversity in cryptography and security.

**Time:** 15:30–16:30  
**Location:** IMC.004

### Identity Protocols and the Evolution of Modern Authentication
**Date:** 12 November 2025  
**Time:** 13:00–14:00  
**Location:** IDL Auditorium


This session welcomed **Jon Harry**, Technical Lead at Okta, to explore the evolution of modern identity protocols—from Single Sign-On, to OAuth for API protection, through to the seamless authentication model enabled by OpenID Connect. The talk highlighted major milestones in secure, user-centric access and examined how modern digital ecosystems have transformed identity management.

**Speaker Bio:**  
**Jon Harry** is a global subject matter expert in **Identity and Access Management** and serves as the **Technical Sales Enablement Lead at Okta**, with a focus on securing AI-driven environments. He specialises in access management, API protection, and federation, and is known for making complex technical concepts accessible through engaging presentations, practical guidance, and hands-on technical advocacy.

## My role in the series
My involvement in the ACE-CSE Cybersecurity Talk Series centres on helping shape the series as a meaningful bridge between academic study and professional cyber security practice.  

- speaker engagement and relationship management
- coordinating with invited experts from academia and industry
- helping frame sessions so they are relevant to student learning and professional development
- supporting the delivery of a talk programme that spans technical, strategic, and career-focused themes
- contributing to a broader cyber security community experience for students at WMG

The series has been especially valuable as a way of bringing external voices into the learning environment and ensuring students encounter a wider range of perspectives on cyber security, from highly technical research topics through to organisational resilience, intelligence, identity, and enterprise risk.
